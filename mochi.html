Mochi (Japan)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour (mochiko)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch (for dusting)
  • 1/2 cup sweet red bean paste (anko)

Introduction

Mochi is a soft, chewy rice cake made from glutinous rice flour, traditionally filled with sweet red bean paste, but also enjoyed with ice cream or fruit.

Instructions

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl, mix glutinous rice flour, sugar, and water until smooth.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 1 minute. Stir, then microwave for another 30 seconds. Repeat until the dough becomes thick and sticky.
  3. Dust a clean surface with cornstarch and transfer the dough onto it. Let it cool slightly.
  4. Divide the dough into small portions, flatten each piece, and place a small amount of red bean paste in the center.
  5. Carefully wrap the dough around the filling, sealing the edges.
  6. Lightly dust with cornstarch and serve.

Cultural Background

Mochi has been enjoyed in Japan for centuries, often eaten during celebrations like the New Year. It symbolizes good fortune and prosperity

Cooking Tip

Dust your hands with cornstarch to prevent the dough from sticking while shaping the mochi.

Substitution

For a different filling, try using chocolate, peanut butter, or fresh strawberries
